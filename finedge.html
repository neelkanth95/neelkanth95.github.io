FinEdge

Smart Credit Scoring & Churn Prevention Engine

"Using behavioral AI to turn high-risk churn signals into 15% incremental revenue"

The Problem

Banks lose 15-20% of credit card customers annually, with the average cost of acquiring a new customer being 5x higher than retaining an existing one. Traditional churn prediction models rely on static demographic data and fail to capture real-time behavioral signals‚Äîmeaning banks only discover a customer is leaving after they've closed their account. This reactive approach costs the financial services industry $136 billion annually in preventable customer attrition.

The Solution

FinEdge is a real-time churn prevention engine that monitors customer behavior across transaction patterns, customer service interactions, and competitive research signals. When high-risk churn indicators are detected, the system automatically triggers personalized retention offers‚Äîloan rate adjustments, cashback incentives, or premium upgrades‚Äîtailored to each customer's financial profile.

  • Behavioral Segmentation: Clusters customers into risk tiers using transaction velocity, credit utilization, and engagement metrics
  • Predictive Churn Scoring: Real-time risk scores updated every 72 hours based on evolving customer behavior
  • Automated Retention Triggers: Dynamic offer engine that personalizes interventions based on customer lifetime value (CLV)

Technical Stack & Implementation

The system architecture combines batch processing for historical analysis with real-time streaming for immediate churn detection:

  • Data Pipeline: Ingests transaction logs, customer service tickets, and credit bureau data via Apache Kafka
  • Feature Engineering: Extracts 120+ behavioral features including transaction velocity, spending category shifts, and customer service escalation patterns
  • Model Architecture: Ensemble of XGBoost (for tabular features) + LSTM (for time-series behavior patterns) achieving 83% AUC-ROC
  • Deployment: Served via FastAPI with Redis caching for sub-200ms inference latency at 10K+ requests/minute

Lessons Learned

Why XGBoost Over Neural Networks for Churn Prediction

Initially tested deep learning approaches (TabNet, FT-Transformer) but found XGBoost outperformed them by 4% in AUC-ROC while being 10x faster to train. The key insight: churn prediction relies heavily on feature interactions (e.g., "declining transaction count + increasing competitor research"), which gradient boosting handles exceptionally well. Neural networks struggled with the small sample size of true churn events (~2-3% of dataset).

The Importance of Temporal Features

Adding LSTM for sequential patterns (e.g., "customer stopped using rewards program 30 days before account closure") improved recall by 12%. This taught me that change patterns are more predictive than static snapshots‚Äîa customer with declining engagement is higher risk than one who was never engaged.

Balancing Precision vs. Recall in Production

The business constraint was clear: false positives (offering retention deals to non-churners) cost money, but false negatives (missing a churner) cost even more. We optimized for recall at 80% precision, accepting that 20% of retention offers would go to "safe" customers‚Äîbut this was cheaper than losing high-value accounts.

User Adoption Requires Change Management

Early pilots showed relationship managers ignoring high-risk alerts because they didn't trust the model. Solution: Added explainability ("This customer's risk is high because...") and ran lunch-and-learn sessions showing successful retention case studies. User adoption jumped from 40% ‚Üí 78% within 2 months.
