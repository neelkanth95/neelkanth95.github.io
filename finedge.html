Solution

1. Brief of the Solution

FinEdge is a real-time churn prevention dashboard that predicts which customers are likely to cancel in the next 30 days and automatically suggests personalized retention offers. It uses machine learning to analyze 120+ behavioral signals (transaction patterns, customer service interactions, engagement metrics) and provides relationship managers with actionable insights.

Core Value Proposition:

"Know which customers are leaving before they do, and take action with one click."

Key Capabilities:

  • Predictive Risk Scoring: Machine learning model (XGBoost + LSTM) generates churn probability for each customer, updated every 72 hours
  • Smart Recommendations: Offer engine suggests optimal retention tactics (APR reduction, cashback, rewards) based on customer segment and CLV
  • One-Click Deployment: Pre-approved offers can be sent via email/SMS instantly‚Äîno 5-day approval process
  • Explainability: Dashboard shows why a customer is at risk (e.g., "Transaction volume dropped 35% in last 30 days")

4. Edge Cases

  • Customer Already Contacted: If RM sends an offer, system should prevent duplicate offers for 30 days
  • Customer Churns Before Offer Sent: If customer cancels before RM acts, mark alert as "Missed Opportunity" and track in analytics

  • Customer Not in Salesforce: If customer record missing in CRM, log error and notify admin (don't crash dashboard)
  • Offer Expires: If customer doesn't respond within 14 days, auto-mark offer as "Expired" and re-evaluate risk score

Key Learnings & Reflections

üí° Start Simple, Then Add Complexity

My first model used 200+ features and took 2 hours to train. I simplified to 50 key features and got the same accuracy in 15 minutes. Lesson: More data doesn't always mean better predictions‚Äîfocus on signal, not noise.

üí° Talk to Users Early and Often

I built a beautiful dashboard that relationship managers never used because it didn't fit their workflow. After shadowing them for a week, I redesigned it around their daily routine and adoption jumped 3x. Lesson: Ship early prototypes and observe real usage.

üí° Change is More Predictive Than Levels

A customer who always spends $5K/month isn't high risk. But a customer who drops from $5K to $2K in 30 days? That's a red flag. The model improved 12% when I added "change over time" features. Lesson: Behavioral deltas > absolute values.

üí° Build Trust with Explanations

Managers didn't trust the AI at first. When I added "why" explanations (e.g., "This customer's risk is high because they called support 3 times in 2 weeks"), they started taking action. Lesson: Explainability is not optional‚Äîit's a core feature.
