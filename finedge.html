FinEdge

Smart Credit Scoring & Churn Prevention Engine

"Using behavioral AI to turn high-risk churn signals into 15% incremental revenue"

The Problem

Banks lose 15-20% of credit card customers annually, with the average cost of acquiring a new customer being 5x higher than retaining an existing one. Traditional churn prediction models rely on static demographic data and fail to capture real-time behavioral signalsâ€”meaning banks only discover a customer is leaving after they've closed their account. This reactive approach costs the financial services industry $136 billion annually in preventable customer attrition.

The Solution

FinEdge is a real-time churn prevention engine that monitors customer behavior across transaction patterns, customer service interactions, and competitive research signals. When high-risk churn indicators are detected, the system automatically triggers personalized retention offersâ€”loan rate adjustments, cashback incentives, or premium upgradesâ€”tailored to each customer's financial profile.

  • Behavioral Segmentation: Clusters customers into risk tiers using transaction velocity, credit utilization, and engagement metrics
  • Predictive Churn Scoring: Real-time risk scores updated every 72 hours based on evolving customer behavior
  • Automated Retention Triggers: Dynamic offer engine that personalizes interventions based on customer lifetime value (CLV)

Product Requirements Document

Executive Summary

Objective: Build a real-time churn prevention system that reduces customer attrition by 15% while maintaining an 80% precision rate for retention offers.

Target Users: Relationship managers at Tier-1 banks managing 500+ high-value credit card accounts ($50K+ annual spend).

Success Criteria: 15% reduction in churn within 6 months, $2M+ incremental revenue per quarter, 72-hour response time for high-risk alerts.

Lessons Learned

Why XGBoost Over Neural Networks for Churn Prediction

Initially tested deep learning approaches (TabNet, FT-Transformer) but found XGBoost outperformed them by 4% in AUC-ROC while being 10x faster to train. The key insight: churn prediction relies heavily on feature interactions (e.g., "declining transaction count + increasing competitor research"), which gradient boosting handles exceptionally well. Neural networks struggled with the small sample size of true churn events (~2-3% of dataset).

The Importance of Temporal Features

Adding LSTM for sequential patterns (e.g., "customer stopped using rewards program 30 days before account closure") improved recall by 12%. This taught me that change patterns are more predictive than static snapshotsâ€”a customer with declining engagement is higher risk than one who was never engaged.

Balancing Precision vs. Recall in Production

The business constraint was clear: false positives (offering retention deals to non-churners) cost money, but false negatives (missing a churner) cost even more. We optimized for recall at 80% precision, accepting that 20% of retention offers would go to "safe" customersâ€”but this was cheaper than losing high-value accounts.
