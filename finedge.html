🎯 Context

FinEdge is a portfolio case study designed to demonstrate how I approach a real-world product problem from first principles. The problem, assumptions, workflows, and metrics are grounded in industry research and common challenges faced by banks and credit card issuers.

I treated this exercise like a real product initiative, starting with the business problem, validating user pain points, defining success metrics, and designing a solution that balances machine learning capability with operational reality.

Why This Problem

Business Perspective

Customer churn in credit cards rarely happens overnight. In most cases, customers show early warning signs weeks in advance: reduced spend, repeated support calls, or disengagement. Despite this, many banks only react once a cancellation request is raised.

Banks lose 15% to 20% of credit card customers annually, costing the financial services industry $136 billion in preventable attrition. The cost of acquiring a new customer is 5 times higher than retaining an existing one, yet retention teams often lack a single, actionable view that combines behavioral signals, customer value, and next best actions.

This leads to delayed interventions and inefficient use of retention budgets.

User Perspective

Relationship Manager (Primary User)

Pain: "I only find out customers are unhappy when they call to cancel. By then, it's too late to save them."

Need: A simple daily view showing which customers need attention today, and why. Early warning system that flags at-risk customers before they churn, with pre-approved retention offers she can send with one click.

VP of Retention (Secondary User)

Pain: "We spend heavily on retention, but I can't clearly see what's working. Are we just giving discounts to customers who would've stayed anyway?"

Need: Visibility into churn trends, ROI, and segment-level effectiveness. Executive dashboard showing retention program performance and which customer segments respond best to which offers.

Success Metrics

Success for FinEdge is not just model accuracy. It's measured by whether users trust the system, use it consistently, and whether it leads to meaningful churn reduction.

  • Primary Outcome: Reduce churn rate from 24% to 20.4% (15% reduction) across pilot segments
  • Adoption: 70% or higher weekly active usage among relationship managers
  • Model Precision: 80% or higher precision with low false positives (under 20%)
  • Operational Fit: Actions taken within existing workflows without disruption
  • Revenue Impact: $2M or more incremental revenue from retained customers

User Research & Validation

For this portfolio project, research insights are simulated but based on patterns observed in similar B2B fintech workflows and industry studies.

Key Insights:

  • Scattered Signals: Early churn indicators exist but are spread across multiple systems (CRM, support tickets, transaction logs)
  • Manual Prioritization: RMs prioritize customers manually without clear risk ranking or customer value context
  • Approval Delays: Retention offers are often delayed 5 to 7 days due to approval processes, missing the intervention window
  • No Feedback Loop: Limited visibility into which retention tactics work for which customer segments
  • Reactive Mindset: Teams only act when customers call to cancel, rather than identifying risk proactively

Problems We Are Solving

Job to Be Done: "When I start my workday, I need to know which customers are at risk of leaving so that I can proactively reach out with the right retention offer before it's too late."

Core Pain Points:

  • Current tools only flag churn after customers cancel
  • No CLV-based prioritization of at-risk customers
  • Manual offer selection with long approval cycles
  • Transaction data, support tickets, and engagement metrics aren't connected

Solution Overview

FinEdge is a decision-support tool that surfaces high-risk customers early, explains why they are at risk, and makes it easy for teams to act.

Rather than replacing human judgment, the product is designed to augment it by prioritizing the right customers and reducing time to action.

Core Value Proposition:

"Know which customers are leaving before they do, and take action with one click."

Key Capabilities:

  • Risk Scoring: Predict churn probability based on 120 or more behavioral signals (transaction patterns, support interactions, engagement metrics). Model updated every 72 hours.
  • Explainability: Clear drivers behind each alert (e.g., "Transaction volume dropped 35% in last 30 days")
  • Actionability: Pre-approved offers sent via email/SMS with one click, eliminating 5-day approval delays
  • Visibility: Executive dashboards showing retention performance, offer acceptance rates, and ROI by segment

Why This Approach

Generic CRM tools (Salesforce Einstein, Pega) weren't designed for banking churn. They lack domain-specific features like APR-based offers or transaction velocity tracking. Building custom allowed optimization for our exact use case while keeping implementation under 4 months.

Core User Stories

For Relationship Managers:

  • See a prioritized list of top 20 highest-risk customers each morning
  • Understand why each customer is flagged as high-risk
  • Send pre-approved retention offers with one click
  • Customize offer details when needed (APR, cashback amount)
  • Track which customers have been contacted

For Retention Leadership:

  • View overall churn trends across all segments
  • See which offers have highest acceptance rates
  • Track ROI of retention programs
  • Identify systemic issues driving churn

Key Acceptance Criteria

High-Risk Customer Dashboard

  • Displays top 20 customers with risk score of 85% or higher
  • Shows Name, Risk Score, CLV, Last Transaction, Predicted Churn Date
  • Opens detail page with full profile and risk indicators on click
  • Loads in under 3 seconds for 500 or more customer dataset
  • Allows filtering by risk level and sorting by CLV or Risk Score

One-Click Offer Deployment

  • Suggests 1 to 3 pre-approved offers based on customer segment
  • Delivers email within 5 minutes of clicking "Send Offer"
  • Tracks status (Sent, Accepted, Declined, Expired)
  • Notifies RM when customer accepts

Edge Cases

  • Duplicate Prevention: System prevents sending multiple offers to same customer within 30 days
  • Missed Opportunities: If customer cancels before action, mark as "Missed" and track in analytics
  • Low Confidence: Risk scores between 80% to 85% show "Medium confidence" warning
  • Missing CRM Data: Log error and notify admin without crashing dashboard
  • Expired Offers: Auto-mark as "Expired" after 14 days and re-evaluate risk

Key Learnings

Clarity Beats Complexity

My first model used 200 or more features and took 2 hours to train. I simplified to 50 key features and got the same accuracy in 15 minutes. Simpler models with clearer explanations were more trusted and more useful than complex black-box predictions.

Workflow Fit Matters

I built a beautiful dashboard that relationship managers never used because it didn't fit their workflow. After shadowing them for a week, I redesigned it around their daily routine. Adoption increased only after aligning the dashboard with how users actually start and structure their day.

Change is More Predictive Than Levels

A customer who always spends $5K per month isn't high risk. But a customer who drops from $5K to $2K in 30 days? That's a red flag. The model improved 12% when I added "change over time" features. Behavioral deltas beat absolute values.

Explainability Drives Action

Managers didn't trust the AI at first. When I added "why" explanations (e.g., "This customer's risk is high because they called support 3 times in 2 weeks"), they started taking action. Users acted more confidently when they understood why a customer was flagged. Trust became a product requirement, not a by-product.
