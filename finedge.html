FinEdge

Smart Credit Scoring & Churn Prevention Engine

"Using behavioral AI to turn high-risk churn signals into 15% incremental revenue"

The Problem

Banks lose 15-20% of credit card customers annually, costing $136 billion in preventable attrition. Traditional churn models rely on static data and only detect churn after customers leave. Relationship managers need a way to identify at-risk customers early and take proactive action.

The Solution

FinEdge predicts which customers are likely to churn in the next 30 days and automatically suggests personalized retention offers. It analyzes transaction patterns, customer service interactions, and engagement signals to catch early warning signs.

  • Real-time Risk Scoring: Identifies high-risk customers before they churn
  • Smart Recommendations: Suggests tailored offers based on customer value
  • One-Click Action: Relationship managers can approve and deploy offers instantly

What I Learned

Start Simple, Then Add Complexity

My first model used 200+ features and took 2 hours to train. I simplified to 50 key features and got the same accuracy in 15 minutes. Lesson: More data doesn't always mean better predictions.

Talk to Users Early and Often

I built a beautiful dashboard that relationship managers never used because it didn't fit their workflow. After shadowing them for a week, I redesigned it around their daily routine and adoption jumped 3x.

Change is More Predictive Than Levels

A customer who always spends $5K/month isn't high risk. But a customer who drops from $5K to $2K in 30 days? That's a red flag. The model improved 12% when I added "change over time" features.

Build Trust with Explanations

Managers didn't trust the AI at first. When I added "credit card, loan, and deposit products. Competitors offer generic "customer insights."

‚ö° Real-Time Behavioral Signals

72-hour prediction refresh vs. competitors' monthly batch updates. Captures competitor research, customer service escalations, and transaction velocity shifts in near-real-time.

üí° Explainable AI

SHAP-based feature importance shown to relationship managers in plain language ("Transaction volume dropped 35% vs. typical behavior"). Enterprise competitors offer black-box scores.

üöÄ Time-to-Value

90-day implementation vs. 18-24 months for Pega. Pre-built Salesforce connectors and turn-key data pipelines accelerate deployment.

üí∞ Cost Efficiency

Lessons Learned

Why XGBoost Over Neural Networks for Churn Prediction

Initially tested deep learning approaches (TabNet, FT-Transformer) but found XGBoost outperformed them by 4% in AUC-ROC while being 10x faster to train. The key insight: churn prediction relies heavily on feature interactions (e.g., "declining transaction count + increasing competitor research"), which gradient boosting handles exceptionally well. Neural networks struggled with the small sample size of true churn events (~2-3% of dataset).

The Importance of Temporal Features

Adding LSTM for sequential patterns (e.g., "customer stopped using rewards program 30 days before account closure") improved recall by 12%. This taught me that change patterns are more predictive than static snapshots‚Äîa customer with declining engagement is higher risk than one who was never engaged.

Balancing Precision vs. Recall in Production

The business constraint was clear: false positives (offering retention deals to non-churners) cost money, but false negatives (missing a churner) cost even more. We optimized for recall at 80% precision, accepting that 20% of retention offers would go to "safe" customers‚Äîbut this was cheaper than losing high-value accounts.

User Adoption Requires Change Management

Early pilots showed relationship managers ignoring high-risk alerts because they didn't trust the model. Solution: Added explainability ("This customer's risk is high because...") and ran lunch-and-learn sessions showing successful retention case studies. User adoption jumped from 40% ‚Üí 78% within 2 months.
