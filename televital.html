🎯 Context

TeleVital is a portfolio case study exploring how to bring affordable healthcare to India's rural villages through a low-bandwidth telehealth platform. The problem, go-to-market strategy, and unit economics are grounded in real-world healthcare access challenges.

This project demonstrates strategic thinking around market entry, distribution channels, and building sustainable unit economics in underserved markets.

Why This Problem

Market Opportunity

India has 70% of its population in rural areas but only 25% of doctors serve these regions. Tier-3 villages face a critical healthcare gap: nearest clinic is 15 to 30km away, specialists are non-existent, and preventable diseases escalate into emergencies due to delayed care.

The market is massive: 900M rural population across 600,000 villages spending ₹3,500 per person per year on out-of-pocket healthcare. Government's National Digital Health Mission creates regulatory tailwinds for digital health solutions.

User Perspective

Rural Families (Primary Users)

Pain: "My child had fever for 3 days. By the time we traveled 25km to the clinic, it became pneumonia. Lost 2 days of wages plus ₹5,000 in hospital costs."

Need: Affordable, instant access to qualified doctors without travel or lost wages.

Rural Doctors (Supply Side)

Pain: Overwhelmed with non-critical cases, no time for serious patients. ASHAs lack medical training to triage effectively.

Need: Platform to handle routine consultations remotely, earning extra income while serving more patients efficiently.

Success Metrics

Success for TeleVital is achieving product-market fit in pilot villages before scaling. The focus is on proving unit economics and sustained usage, not vanity metrics.

  • User Acquisition: 10,000 registered users across 100 pilot villages
  • Active Usage: 40% monthly active rate (4,000 MAU)
  • Consultation Volume: 5,000+ consultations in first 6 months
  • Unit Economics: CAC:LTV ratio of 1:3 or better (₹45 CAC, ₹1,800 LTV)
  • Customer Satisfaction: NPS of 50 or higher

Guardrails

These metrics should not be negatively affected:

  • Patient Safety: Misdiagnosis rate must remain under 2%
  • Accessibility: App crash rate under 1% on low-end devices
  • Doctor Availability: 80% or higher of consult requests filled within 30 minutes
  • Data Privacy: 100% adherence to NDHM standards

Solution Overview

TeleVital is a low-bandwidth telehealth app optimized for 2G/3G networks. It connects patients with MBBS doctors via video or audio calls in 10+ regional languages, with prescriptions delivered to local pharmacies.

The product is designed for rural constraints: adaptive video that switches to audio-only on poor connectivity, SMS reminders that work on basic feature phones, and offline health records that sync when online.

Core Value Proposition:

"See a qualified doctor from your home in 15 minutes. No travel, no waiting rooms, no lost wages."

Key Features (MVP):

  • Adaptive Calls: Auto-switches to audio-only on poor connectivity to prevent call drops
  • Multilingual: Hindi, Tamil, Telugu, Bengali, Marathi, Gujarati, Kannada, Odia, Punjabi, Malayalam
  • SMS-Based: Appointment reminders work without smartphones via basic SMS
  • Pharmacy Network: Prescription auto-sent to nearest empaneled pharmacy for pickup/delivery
  • Offline Records: Stores basic patient history locally, syncs when online

Go-to-Market Strategy

Distribution Channels

In rural markets, distribution matters more than product. Digital marketing has under 5% reach in Tier-3 villages. Success requires physical presence and trusted intermediaries.

Channel Strategy CAC Estimate
NGO Partnerships Leverage existing community health programs (Pratham, SELCO Foundation) ₹20 to 30/user
ASHA Worker Network Train 200 health volunteers as brand ambassadors with ₹500/month stipend ₹40 to 50/user
Pharmacy Tie-Ups Co-marketing with 50 rural pharmacies (posters, flyers at checkout) ₹30 to 40/user
Village Councils Presentations at panchayat meetings with sarpanch endorsements ₹15 to 25/user

Core User Stories

For Patients:

  • Describe symptoms using text or voice input in local language
  • Receive AI-powered triage (urgent vs routine)
  • Select doctor based on specialty and availability
  • Complete consultation via adaptive video/audio
  • Receive prescription at nearest pharmacy

For Doctors:

  • View pending consultation queue with patient history
  • Conduct consultations with built-in prescription editor
  • Auto-forward prescriptions to pharmacies
  • Track earnings and patient feedback

Key Learnings

Distribution Beats Product in Rural Markets

Building a great app is only 30% of the battle. The real challenge is distribution. Getting the app into users' hands requires physical presence through NGO partnerships and ASHA workers. Digital marketing has minimal reach in these areas.

Freemium Requires Trust-Building First

Initial strategy was pure freemium. User research revealed rural users won't pay upfront without proof. Pivot: first consult free, then ₹99/month subscription. This "try before you buy" model increased conversion 4x.

SMS Beats Push Notifications

Push notifications require constant internet. SMS works on basic ₹500 feature phones and has 95% delivery rate. For appointment reminders and prescription alerts, SMS is non-negotiable in rural contexts.

Pharmacy Partnership Creates Offline Moat

Integrating with local pharmacies creates a physical touchpoint that digital-only competitors can't replicate. When a patient picks up medicine, the pharmacy reinforces TeleVital's brand, creating a virtuous cycle of trust and retention.
