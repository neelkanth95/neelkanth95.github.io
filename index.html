What I Do

My journey into product management started with curiosity about how ideas turn into real products. While exploring product case studies and research papers, I began prototyping my own product ideas, which helped me understand that great products are built on clarity of problem, structured decisions, and deep user understanding, not just strong technology.

At Turing, I worked as a Research Analyst on AI and LLM-based products. I was involved in defining evaluation rubrics, quality requirements, and success metrics for LLM and deep research projects in the media domain. I worked closely with engineers, quality teams, and stakeholders to improve workflows, track KPIs, and deliver measurable improvements in productivity and output quality.

Over time, I evolved from execution to product thinking, identifying user pain points, prioritizing improvements, building KPI dashboards, and using data to guide decisions. Alongside this, I’m actively learning UI/UX design and experimentation to better understand user behavior, interface choices, and A/B testing. My goal is to grow as a product manager who combines AI/ML understanding, analytical depth, Business knowlegde and strong user empathy to build meaningful products.
