JoshTalks AI: Humanness Platform

Designing a mobile-first crowdsourced visual database to train culturally inclusive Computer Vision models, alongside an automated behavior-based quality detection system.

Company: JoshTalks AI • Timeline: Nov 2024 • Target Audience: Rural Contributors

1. Initiative Overview

Current AI vision models struggle to identify culturally specific Indian scenes, such as Durga Puja pandals, village hand pumps, bullock carts, and rural schools. Because these models are trained primarily on Western datasets, they fail to serve the 1.4 billion people of India effectively.

This take-home product management assignment for JoshTalks AI addresses this gap through two main initiatives:

Platform Scope & Focus

  • Part 1: Mobile Crowdsourcing PWA: Enabling rural contributors in low-connectivity areas (2G/3G) to upload authentic images with multilingual text or voice descriptions.
  • Part 2: Automated Quality Detection System: Designing a tracking algorithm to detect and warn users of low-effort or fraudulent work based on behavioral signals.

2. Strategic Alignment & Objective

The "Humanness Platform" bridges the gap between raw AI model capacity and real-world applicability by collecting authentic data directly from local environments.

Business Impact (JoshTalks AI)

  • Scalability: Establish a path to collect 1M+ verified images across India's 700+ districts.
  • Competitiveness: Position JoshTalks AI as the premier provider of culturally representative datasets.
  • Applicability: Power inclusive AI solutions in agriculture, rural diagnostics, and localized education.

User Personas

👤 Rural Contributors (Primary)

"AI knows the Eiffel Tower perfectly, but doesn't recognize a village hand pump or local festival."

Local volunteers, NGO field workers, and community leaders who want to see their environment represented and earn rewards for authentic visual contributions.

👥 Internal Reviewers & Admins (Secondary)

Internal operations teams who need to evaluate dataset submissions, verify location coordinates, flag duplicate uploads, and ensure overall dataset hygiene.

3. Core Technical Challenges

Challenge 1: Patchy Connectivity (2G/3G)

Rural areas suffer from unstable network connections, making direct image uploads unreliable.

Solution: Offline-first architecture. Submissions are stored locally in IndexedDB and queued for background synchronization once a stable connection is detected.

Challenge 2: Low-End Hardware

Target users typically use basic Android smartphones with limited RAM (1-2GB) and storage constraints.

Solution: Built as a lightweight Progressive Web App (PWA) rather than a native app, utilizing aggressive client-side image compression before syncing.

Challenge 3: Low Literacy and Language Barriers

Many potential contributors speak regional Indian languages and find typing detailed descriptions difficult.

Solution: Voice-to-text inputs in 10+ major Indian languages, making input intuitive and accessible.

5. Automated Quality Detection (Part 2)

In crowdsourcing models, some users inevitably submit low-effort or automated work to speed up payouts. Based on sample transcription datasets, the following four low-quality patterns were defined for automated detection:

Behavioral Pattern Detection Logic Action Trigger
Incomplete Listening time_taken < (0.5 × duration) AND text < 4 words Fails to listen to audio before submission
Copy-Paste / AI Generated chars_per_sec > 15 OR identical to Whisper baseline Automatic text entry without manual editing
False Blank Submissions text == "blank" (or empty) AND duration > 2 seconds User skips listening to avoid transcribing
Idle Engagement chars_per_sec < 0.5 AND time_taken > (2 × duration) User opens task and leaves it active without working

Behavior-Based Quality Scoring

Instead of banning users immediately after a single flag, the platform computes a rolling Quality Score over their last 20 submissions:

Quality Score = 100 - (10 × Total Flags in Last 20 Tasks)

Score Tiers:

  • 80 to 100: Safe Tier (Auto-approve payments)
  • 50 to 70: Warning Tier (Soft nudge warning shown to user)
  • Under 50: Audit Tier (Pushes account to manual moderator review)

Progressive Escalation System

  1. Stage 1 (Soft Warning): In-app educational notification advising the user to slow down and listen.
  2. Stage 2 (Temporary Hold): Account paused for 24 hours. The contributor must complete a quick training module to resume.
  3. Stage 3 (Manual Audit): Moderator reviews 3-5 random clips manually to verify authenticity.
  4. Stage 4 (Permanent Suspension): Account deactivated if fraudulent behavior persists (e.g. repeated typing speed >15 chars/sec).

6. Measuring Success

We track OKRs across both regional coverage and review operations efficiency:

Key Metric Target Threshold Strategic Value
Verified Images per District ≥ 1,000 images Ensures visual diversity across different contexts
Village Representation ≥ 90% (540K+ villages) Ensures dataset isn't biased towards semi-urban zones
Average Moderation Turnaround < 48 hours Creates a fast feedback and payment loop for users
User Rejection Rate < 15% Ensures clear onboarding and low user frustration

7. Key Learnings & Reflections

💡 Design for Extreme Realities

Assuming standard 4G connectivity is a common pitfall. Designing offline-first models proved that thinking through technical limitations early creates highly resilient systems.

💡 Accessibility is a Feature

Introducing native voice translation elements makes the platform accessible to a wider pool of contributors, ensuring a more diverse dataset.
